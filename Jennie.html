Her early life

  Jennie Kim was born on January 16, 1996, in Cheongdam-dong, Gangnam-gu, Seoul. Being the only child, she attended Cheongdam Elementary School in Seoul before moving to New Zealand where she had a life-changing experience. When she was eight, she visited Australia and New Zealand with her family. Moreover, after expressing her fondness for New Zealand, she was sent to study at Waikowhai Intermediate School in Auckland and lived with a homestay family.

  Jennie’s experience learning a new language was documented on MBC’s English, Must Change to Survive in 2006. During her teenage years, she dreamed of becoming a ballet dancer. After finishing intermediate, she enrolled at ACG Parnell College.

  It was in New Zealand where Jennie first discovered K-pop and developed an interest in YG Entertainment’s music. However, her mother planned to move her to Florida to continue her studies to become a lawyer or teacher when she turned 14. Jennie wasn’t thrilled with the idea, and instead, she moved back to South Korea in 2010 to study at Cheongdam Middle School. Her family supported her decision, and that same year, Jennie auditioned for YG Entertainment with Rihanna’s “Take a Bow” and was accepted as a trainee.

Career

  Jennie began her career as a trainee under YG Entertainment in 2010. And spent six years honing her skills before she was finally announced as a member of BLACKPINK in 2016. She quickly became known for her powerful rapping and charismatic stage presence. And helped to establish BLACKPINK as one of the most popular and successful K-pop groups of all time.

Jennie’s shyness and hidden talent as a rapper

  Although Jennie struggled with introducing herself and was hesitant to take initiative due to her shyness, the company saw potential in her as a rapper since most of the songs she covered included raps, and she was the only trainee fluent in English and Korean at the time. She also knows basic Japanese and has learned French.

Television Appearances of Jennie

  Jennie has also appeared in several television shows, including “Village Survival, the Eight” and “Running Man”. In addition, she has released several solo songs, including “SOLO,” which was a commercial success and topped music charts in South Korea and abroad.


Solo Debut Album by Jennie

  "Solo" (stylized in all caps) is the debut solo single of South Korean singer, rapper, and Blackpink member Jennie. Released on November 12, 2018, through YG and Interscope, the song was written and produced by Teddy and 24. Musically, "Solo" is a dance, pop, and hip hop song with EDM elements. Its lyrical content revolves around the theme of independence after a break-up.

  "You & Me" is a song by South Korean singer, rapper, and Blackpink member Jennie. It was released on October 6, 2023, through YG Entertainment and Interscope Records, as a special single after being performed on Blackpink's Born Pink World Tour (2022–2023). It marked Jennie's first solo single in five years after "Solo" (2018).
